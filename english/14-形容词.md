# 形容词

---

- 形容词(adj.)是词类的一种, 主要用来描写或修饰名词或代词, 表示人或事物的性质, 状态, 特征或属性
- This is a book.
- This is a **cheap** book.
- This is an **expensive** book.

## 一.adjective + noun

- Is't a nice day today.
- Laura has got blue eyes.
- I don't speak any foreign languages.
- There are some beautiful flowers.

- a different place
- different places (no differents) 形容词不变

## 二.be + adjective

- The weather is nice today.
- These flowers are beautiful.
- I'm hungry. Can I have something to eat?
- Please be quiet. I'm reading.

## 三.感官动词 + adjective

- You look tired.
- You sound happy.
- It smells good.
- It tastes good.

## 四.比较级和最高级

- 在英语中表示 比较... 和 最... 时, 形容词要用特别的形式, 称为比较级和最高级, 原来的形容词成为原级

1. 单音节和少数双音节词，加-er 和-est
   - young - younger - youngest
   - clean - cleaner - cleanest
2. 以 e 结尾的词，加-r 和-st
   - large - larger - largest
   - wide - wider - widest
   - nice - nicer - nicest
3. 以辅音+y 结尾，变 y 为 i，再加-er 和-est
   - busy - busider - busiest
   - easy - easier - easiest
   - heavy - heavier - heaviest
4. 以重读闭音节结尾(辅元辅，三个字母结尾)，双写最后一个字符，再加-er 和-est
   - big - bigger - biggest
   - fat - fatter - fattest
   - hot - hotter - hottest
5. 其他双音节和多音节的词，在前面加 more 和 most
   - important - more important - most important
   - difficult - more different - most different
   - interesting —> more interesting - most interesting
   - useful - more useful - most useful
6. 不规则
   - good/well - better - best
   - bad/ill - worse - worst
   - little/few - less - lease
   - much/many - more - most
   - far - farther(指距离)/further(指程度) - farthest(指距离)/furthest(指程度)
   - old - older(年龄, 新旧)/elder(血缘上的) - oldest(年龄, 新旧)/eldest(血缘上的)
7. 注意事项
   - 形容词最高级前通常必须用定冠词 the, 副词最高级可不用
   - the + 最高级 + n
   - The sahare is **the** biggest desert in the world!
   - most 前面没有 the, 不表示最高级的含义, 只表示非常(自己不会使用)
   - It is a **most important** problem.
   - It is a **very important** problem.

## 五.形容词的用法

1. 写句子时, 比较级常用 then
   - 对象 + be + 比较级 + then + 比较对象
   - His room is bigger than mine.
   - She is younger than my sister.
   - 比较程度
   - a bit - much
   - Canada is much bigger than France.
   - Sue is a bit older then Gary. She' is 25 and he' 24.
   - the + 最高级 (+ 可选的名词)
   - The ASCII of z is bigger than all the other ASCII of letter.
   - The ASCII of z is the biggest ASCII of letter.
2. 形容词同级比较
   - 由 as(so)... as 引出, 其否定式为 not so... 或 not as...as
   - (not) as + 形容词原型 + as
   - She's old, but she' not as old as he is.
   - not as much as... / not as many as...
   - 前者用于不可数名词
   - I haven't got as much money as you.
   - I don't know as many people as you.
   - the same as.
   - the same (+n.) + as + 对象
   - The weather today is the same as yesterday.
3. 表示倍数的比较级
   - 用 twice(two times), four times, ten times + as ... as 结构
   - 数字 + times + as + 原级 + as
   - This one is four times as big as that one.
   - Our campus is three times as large as yours.
   - 数字 + times + as + 比较级 + than
   - This one is three times as bigger than one.
   - Our campus is two times as larger than yours.
4. 常用短语
   - as such 表示上文所指明的事或人
   - He is child, and must be treated as such.
   - as much 同量, 用于不可数名词
   - Take as much as you like.
   - I would gladly have paid twice as much for it.
   - as many 一样多, 用于可数名词
   - I found six mistakes in as many lines.
   - no more than 只不过, 两者都不, 等于 not any more than
   - Man cannot live without food any more than plants can grow without Sunshine.
   - no less than 不亚于
   - There are no less than five hundred people present at the New Year party. 强调人多
   - not less than 不少于
   - There are not less than five hundred people present at the New Year party. 强调人不多
   - more often than not 表示多数情况下
   - On friday mornings, he comes late more often than not.
   - all the more 因而更加
   - We really admired him all the more for his frankness.
   - lettle more than 差不多
   - The grain in their barn is little more than that of ours.
   - more or less 或多或少
   - The work is more or less finished.
   - other than 除了
   - They imposed no precondition other than that the meeting should be held in their capital.
   - rather than 而不是
   - Read what interests you; read what you have time for magazines and newspapers rather then novels.
   - so much the better 就更好了
   - If she will help us, so much than better.
   - so much than worse 就更糟了
   - So much the worse for you if you still are absent form class.
   - the more ... the more(less)越是(少)... 就越...
   - The more water you give to the plants, the more beaftiful they will be.

## 六.特殊

- 并非所有的形容词和副词都有比较级和最高级, 如极限形容词和非程度形容词
- 极限形容词
- perfect, unique, impossible, empty, full, worthless
- 非程度形容词
- 表示国籍, 颜色, 方向等
- Chinese, blue, left, monthly

## 七.形容词句式

- 形容词 + 介词 + 名词(或代词或动名词)
- I am not interesting in playing computer games.
- 形容词 + 不定式
- She is sure to pass the exam.
- 形容词 + enough + 不定式
- The boy is old enough to go school.
- too + 形容词 + 不定式
- The boy is too young to go to school.
- so + 形容词 + as + 不定式
- We have been so unfortunate as to miss him.
